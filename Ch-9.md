<h3>Chapter Nine</h3>
<h3>Open hands. Empty hands.</h3>
</center>



<center>----------</center><br>

‘Cockadoo… Cockadoo!’

I sat bolt upright in the bed. I wasn't hung over, but like hell was I going to put up with that at the break of dawn.

'No! Be quiet!' I pointed a finger at him, sitting on the windowsill, ready to crow.

_I guess that's what you call a Mexican Standoff..._

I threatened him with puny lightning and he held my ears hostage.

'How about you be quiet and I give you some food?' I tried, and slowly got up from the bed.

I reached into the grain pouch I left on the table and threw a handful in his direction. We watched each other intently, and I really hoped he wouldn't call my bluff. After a long, tense moment, he deflated, hopped down and started to peck at the grain.

I wasn't going to be able to sleep, all riled up as I was, so I decided to get up and get dressed. I went to the study, see what books there were, maybe some magical tomes?

I was surprised see Tiffany there, sprawled over an armchair, book in hand. She looked like she hasn't slept much. Or at all.

'Uh... Good Morning. Are you alright?'

She didn't look very happy to see me.

'No. I never sleep when there are strangers in the house.' She answered, annoyed. I decided this wasn't the time to be asking questions.

'I'll just grab a book to read and I will be out of your hair.' 

I moved in quickly, trying to find something interesting and leave. There was a full shelf of The Merry Bunch, with a few I haven't read yet. Easy choice.

'You like them too, huh?' The storm cloud that was Tiffany's face, brightened up a bit.

'It's my favourite light read, even though Dad says they're silly.'

'So what? They end well, and that's what I want from them. Have you read the practical advice at the back?'

'I have. It's wild hocus-pocus, written to be entertaining in the same vein as the story.'

'Mhm.' She grunted noncommitally. I took that as my cue to leave.

<center>----------</center><br>

After breakfast, Tiffany invited me on a walk. Robbie was adamant to come with, until she told him we would be discussing boys and kissing. We got the cutest 'Ewwww!' in history.

As we left, I noticed she was wearing trousers. I had nothing against it, but it wasn't a common choice. I guessed in this household many things were done differently.

We walked for a while in awkward silence, but I wasn't too bothered, everything around was new and unfamiliar. So much to see. We ended up in the park bordering the main square and sat at a secluded bench. She finally asked the question that was on her mind.

'Why are you here?' She fired.

I wasn't ready for this one.

'Well, Decker told me to come here...' I thought about it. 'We were shaken, my Dad and I, coming here promised answers. I just had my... OUCH.' I spat the final word out. 

She laughed at that, even snorted a few times. For a moment I was offended, but the absurdity of the name hit me too, and I joined her. We settled after a moment, the mood somewhat lighter. Then she became serious again.

'Just don't ruin it for us, please?' She asked in a tiny voice. 'We have nowhere else to go.'

My chest tightened. I suspected there was a story there, and not a fun one... I had to be very careful.

'Do you wish to tell me? I am here to listen, if you need me to.' I smiled at her, hopefully as gently as I thought I did. 

'I... No... Not now... Sorry.' 

I got offended, I have shown her only goodwill and she treated me like some casual acquaintance.  
Which I was.  
This was a moment to be strong for someone else.  

_Thank you, Dad, for showing me how._ 

'No, I am sorry for asking. I did not realize the weight of it. If you ever trust me enough to tell, I will be there, ok?'

'I don't know. Maybe. I'll think about it.'

'I can't ask for more. If and whenever you are ready.' She looked at me like she was expecting me to press further. I didn't.

'Can you tell me more of what Travis and Decker are like?' I needed a second opinion.

She hesitated and thought for a moment.

'I don't know very much myself, these two are very tight-lipped about what they go about doing. I know neither of these two have jobs like most people, and that they're waist deep in... our world.'

'It must pay pretty well, both seem very well off?'

'I guess? I know Sean works for some big company and makes good money.' 

I didn't recognize the name, so I asked. 

'I forgot you haven't met him yet.' Tiffany looked at me closely as she continued. 'He is Travis's partner...' 

_Oooohkayyyy..._

'Like... a husband?' I decided I had to be sure.

'Yes, but we do not talk to people about it. I'm only telling you because you would figure it out eventually. Should save you an awkward moment.' I could see her blush a little.

'By the way, don't expect it to be easy here. There are...expectations.' She said very seriously.

'Expectations? What kind of expectations?' I asked carefully.

She nodded slowly. I dreaded her next words.

Then she burst out laughing.

'You should have seen your face!'

_Why you little..._

'You spend too much time around these two! You'll turn into a gremlin like them!' 

I felt the tension escape through the shared laugh, as I pinched the bridge of my nose. She continued once we could focus again.

'But seriously. They'll make you read books you might not want to, or make you go shoot a gun. God forbid Decker decides it's time for a fencing lesson. You'll get told to do shopping or cook, maybe help at Utopia.'

'That's not too bad.'

'It's not. But it's hard. But not too hard.' She still smiled though.
