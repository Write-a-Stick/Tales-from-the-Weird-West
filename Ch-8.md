<h3>Chapter Eight</h3>
<h3>The rainbow you don't expect</h3>
</center>

The house had a study, exactly like you would imagine it. Fireplace, four leather armchairs and wooden bookshelves lining every wall. Add a pair of floor-to-ceiling windows, and because Travis lived here, several crystal decanters with an assortment of glasses on a wheeled table.

I didn't have time to peruse the book selection before we sat down, but it looked promising. We all took a chair, apart from Robbie, who snuggled in Tiffany's lap.

The lecture was brief, but thorough. Sort of a primer to the supernatural. Travis took the lead.

'We are Outists, as you know, but not every one of us is a "Caster". Both are catch-all terms. You can't really tell us apart just by looking, and regular folks stand no chance at all. Physical effects are visible, of course, but I'm talking about "spells" themselves. Let me show you.'

He took out a deck of cards, shuffled like he did at our first meeting and pointed at the fireplace. The logs and leftover coals inside burst into flames. _Pretty impressive._

'I see. So I have to be really wary of anyone shuffling cards? That can't be it...' I thought for a moment. 'When I saw Decker do his magic on the hill, I saw golden sparkles before anything happened?'

'You didn't see anything between the shuffle and the fireplace?' Travis asked and I shook my head.

'You should have seen bright green arcs... We'll need to figure it out sharp, you can't be walking around blind.'

The lecture continued.

Magic is 'cast', effects are called 'spells', and you 'cast' them.  
You cannot recognize a 'Spell-Caster' until he or she is actively using magic.  
Regular folks see nothing or adjust.

'Wait, if people can't see it, are the real effects that impactful? Is it that dangerous to them? Is this why...'

Then a chill went through me as I remembered Dad's blank stare...

'I understand.'

Magic is color-coded, and there was quite a list.  
Golden for Shamans.  
Bright green for Hucksters.  
White for those whom God responds to.  
Bluish for natural talent, like Tiffany or I. 
Purple for Voodoo. 
Red for demonic. 

_So God and demons exist... Oooohkayyyyy..._

Then there was black.

'Black was the color of ancient, indescribable creatures born of the Abyss between worlds. If you see black, run.'

They were very serious when they mentioned black. These two aren't serious often, that in of itself was chilling.

Finally, they explained me, to me. Apparently, I could do all sorts of things, but they all have to be rooted and connected to a memory, a very emotional one.

'You don't learn new spells like we do. They sort of happen to you. If you make yourself feel that again, the spell will work. Mind, this is book knowledge on a very obscure topic.'

'So you're telling me that to cast a puny lightning, I have to feel the same headache, frustration at the inevitable and my hatred of Mondays?'

That combination sounded impossible to recreate.

'Not exactly. All magic takes practice, like everything. You got a puny lightning, because you don't know what you're doing, yet. In time, I expect it to be enough to kill an ox.'

'Ummmm... I don't think I want to run around killing things...' I started uneasily.

Decker decided to throw his own three cents into the hat.

'Good, that should always be a last resort. Always, because you can't ever take it back.' 

He locked eyes with me. 

'You better be sure. Every. Single. time.'

This was suspiciously deep for McCoy. Personal. I decided to remember it.

'Just remember that sometimes what you want, what is right and what you have to do can be four different things.' Travis said grimly.

'That's three, if I decide to be generous...' 

_Take that._

'No, four. You forgot the outcome. Sometimes you can be right, do everything right and things still go to shit.'
