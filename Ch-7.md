<h3>Chapter Seven</h3>
<h3>Mistakes were made</h3>
</center>

_I'm never drinking again._

It wasn't the first time I suffered the aftereffects, however that morning after deciding to stay in Tucson was particularly vicious. I was parched and the world was spinning, regardless if I had my eyes open or not. Then there was the headache.

I desperately needed water, so I sat up in the bed and looked around the unfamiliar room. Some kind soul put a pitcher and glass on the nightstand. I refilled my glass twice before I decided the room stopped spinning enough to properly look around.

I had a vague memory of someone mentioning a house attached to Utopia, I figured it was where I found myself. I was, most likely, in a guest room. Pretty well appointed, the furniture was good quality and pretty new. 

Then I noticed You Bastard, perched on the chair. He had one eye open, the reflections off my glass must have woken him.

You Bastard is a rooster.  
It was morning.  
And I was in a room with him.

He took a deep breath.

_Oh no._

'Cadaa...'

What you need to understand about You Bastard is that on Mondays... he crows differently. Somehow, it is much, much worse than the other days.

_I hate Mondays_

'Cadooo...'

_Here we go_

'CADOOLADOOOOODOOOO!!!'

I knew it was coming and I couldn't let it just happen. In an act of painful defiance, I screamed with and back at him.

'I HAAATE MONDAAAYS!!!

The skull-splitting headache crescendoed. I felt my hair stand on end and my skin crawl with invisible insects. There was a loud bang, and from my ourstretched hand, a small lightning connected with You Bastard.

_Holy Crap! I did magic!_

I was shocked. The rooster went quiet. Dead quiet.

Then we both noticed that the lightning only slightly singed his foot. His beady little eyes narrowed. I glanced at the distance to the door. I could make it.

'BWAAAAAAAAAAAAK!'

I dashed for the door, my stomach protesting at the rapid movement. I could hear You Bastard launching himself after me. I shut the door just in time to hear him scratch and peck at the door. He was always a feisty one.

'I'm sorry, OK? I didn't know it was going to happen!' I tried explaining to the door.

'BWAAAK! BWAAK POCK!' More scratching and pecking.

'How about I get you some bread to say I'm sorry?'

'Cabwaak...' I couldn't believe I was negotiating with a chicken. Which I technically owned. And it was my pet, not the other way around.

'Cabwaak! Bock Bock!' That sounded like an agreement.

This was the moment another door in the hall opened and a head peeked out. It was a boy, around ten, round faced, with blond hair sticking out in every direction. He looked a bit sleepy as he came towards me.

I was lucky the ruckus woke only him. I didn't want to imagine what the world was like for someone in close proximity to not one, but two unhinged, hung-over wizards.

'Hi there... I'm Emma.' I said quietly to him and waved a little. 

'Hi... I'm Robbie. Are you going to be our sister now?'

That is not the kind of question you want to hear in the state I was in. I wasn't ready. 

'Ummm... No?'

'Oh... I thought our dads brought someone else to live with us...' He looked a bit sad now. _Dads?_

'I will be staying around, not sure if here though.'

Robbie immediately brightened up.

'Friends! I have snacks, want some?' He grabbed my hand without asking and led me to his room. He was so earnest I couldn't refuse.

It was similar to the one I woke up in, except that the bed was a bunk bed, the lower occupied by a blonde girl of around sixteen or seventeen, so a few years younger than me. She didn't look very happy to see me.

'Robbieeeeeee...' She groaned. 'How many times do I need to tell you not to bring people to our room?'

I mouthed a 'Sorry!' and started to back up. 

'Wait!' Timmy opened the bottom drawer of a dresser. It was half-full of cans and waxed paper packages. 

It wasn't a stash of snacks... It was enough food for those two for a week...

He quietly took out a tin of hard candy, opened it and offered me some. I took one and put it in my mouth, even though sweets this early in the morning and in the state I as in weren't the best idea. What else was I going to do though?

'I don't suppose you have a bit of bread? I promised some to my rooster, I need to placate him. He is a bit miffed.' I smiled apologetically.

'I do!' Robbie brightened up and took out two large crackers from one of the waxed packets.

'Thank you.' I waved and backed out quietly and shut the door behind me.

There was a mirror in the hall, which reminded me of my sorry state. Frizzed hair, bags under my eyes and crumpled clothing. I decided I couldn't deal with it right now. I needed to go back to bed.

Cautiously, I slid one of the crackers under the door. It was immediately obliterated in a flurry of pecks, some crumbs rolling back outside.

'Pock! Bwaak!'

I cracked the door open, searching for any sign of the poultry menace that was You Bastard. I found him perched on the chair non-threateningly, eyes closed. 

It was safe to go back to bed. And I was in no state to ponder anything just yet.

It was midday by the time I scraped myself off the bed. I was in desperate need of a toilet. I opened the door to see the girl from before, ready to knock.

'Uh... Morning. You're Emma, right?' She asked.

'Yes, sure, whatever. I need the bathroom, please.' She heard the urgency in my voice and moved aside.

'Last door on the left.' She pointed down the short corridor.

Once I finished marvelling at indoor plumbing in such a remote place, with hot water on tap no less, I found the girl waiting at the staircase.

'Sorry about that. I'm Emma, nice to meet you?' 

'I'm Tiffany, breakfast is ready.'

I decided, with loud input of my grumbling stomach, that hairstyling can wait. I followed Tiffany downstairs, to the kitchen. There were great aromas coming from ahead. I opened the door.

The kitchen was smaller than at The Principal, but pretty spacious for a home. At the stove, Travis was frying bacon and cutting freshly baked bread, looking like last night never happened to him. At the table, Robbie on one side, happily devouring his food. On the other, Decker holding a wet cloth to his face and looking crumpled. He looked like I felt.

'Morning sleepyhead!' Travis exclaimed cheerfully. I could swear it was on purpose.  

Decker groaned.  
I groaned.  
Tiffany laughed.  
We groaned again.

I sat down in the free seat between Tiffany and Decker, enjoying the cool breeze from the open window. Travis filled a plate and set sat in front of me, along with a mug of steaming coffee. I felt good enough to eat, so I carefully picked at it and chased it with the hot brew. Generally there was a companionable silence only interspersed with minor grunts and pained groans.

After we were finished, the dead came back to life, myself included.

'I heard you met Tiff and Robbie earlier?' Travis started. I saw Tiffany glare at him, her face was hilarious. That blend of a pout and I-would-stab-you-with-a-spoon-if-I-didn't-need-you on her young face was just too cute. I couldn't help but laugh.

And then the laugh died on my lips...

_Hold up... Is that what I look like when Dad calls me "Daughter Mine"?_

The irony wasn't lost on me.
