<h3>Chapter Ten</h3>
<h3>Out of the blue, into the pale</h3>
</center>

Tiffany and I wandered the town a little, she was showing me main roads and the biggest local institutions. It was a nice time, the weather staying not too hot and not too cold.

'That's the bank, as you can see. I hope to have business in there one day.' The building front was impressive, but ultimately uninteresting to me right then.

We moved on a couple of streets.

'That’s the ASI building. You’ll see them around town, they also drink at the Utopia. Kind of the reason we don’t have any fights in there.' 

She pointed at a non-descript building, with a bland 'Arizona Special Investigations' plaque on a bland facade. The only thing of interest were two armed men at the front of the building, just sort of lounging at a table near the door. They lazily watched the street and drank coffee.

'Convenient...' And suspicious. From what I read, that's what a criminal front looks like.

_OH... Decker and Travis. Secretive. No jobs. Good money. The mysterious Sean. What have I gotten myself into?_

'... Nevermind. Just a weird thought.' I finished, and glanced at Tiffany, but she just shrugged.

Next, she showed me the market. Storefronts lined a town square, with colourful stalls selling seasonal goods, craftsmen displaying their work and various stands offering hot food. It was lively and smelled amazing, but at this time it was packed with people, so we just skimmed the edge. We stopped by a stall selling baubles and knick-knacks, a gaudily dressed man attending it.  

There was no-one right next to the stall.

I was admiring an intricately carved wooden hairbrush, the vine pattern on the handle was particularly eye-catching, when suddenly Tiffany stiffened, looked around and said something I didn’t really want to hear.

'Something's off...'

As soon as I started paying attention, suspicion rising in me, I felt it too. The feeling of non-descript wrongness...

I started looking around and saw a man standing next to the stall, where before there was no-one. He wasn't very tall, not much taller than me. Asian, about 50 years old. He was bald, with a long grey mustache bordering his mouth and ending below the jaw. Dressed in a striking, long blue tunic and a pair of odd, cloth, black shoes. He met my eyes and looked surprised for a moment, before his face settled back into a neutral expression.  

He used the walking stick he was leaning on to partly point at the stall we were at, and my gaze naturally followed.

I was admiring an intricately carved wooden hairbrush, the vine pattern on the handle was particularly eye-catching...

_Hold up. Something’s off..._

I started looking around and saw a man standing next to the stall, where before there was no-one. He wasn't very tall...

_Wait, I saw him before somewhere..._

'Punch to the ribs. Knife to the throat. Dead.' A voice spoke quietly in my ear. I startled and turned around. McCoy. Of course...

I completely missed the most conspicuous person in the city sneaking up on us, distracted by his shenanigans at the stall. He was grinning, he knew what he did, and now I knew he did it on purpose.

'Did you tell her the rules, Tiffany?' He asked her, I decided to answer instead. 

'No, she didn't. What rules would those be?' I was cautious, I couldn't get the thought of possibly being in collusion with criminals go.

'Tiffany, you heard this already, so you can either stay or go, I'll leave you to decide.' He started walking to a nearby cafe.

'I'll stay.' She said, and we followed toward the aromas of tea and coffee.

The cafe was spacious and pretty busy, filled with quiet conversations and the clinking of cups on saucers. We sat down and looked at the menu. Teas, coffees, cakes and biscuits. Normal. Then there was 'iced coffee' and 'iced tea'.

'Why does everything in this town have ice in it? Ice cream, alcohol and now this? And at prices that seem very low for importing ice to a desert.' I asked. 

Tiffany answered that one.

'Actually, there's an ice factory and a icebox factory in town. Chilling Cubes and Tucson Thermals, I think they're called. I was going to show you later, pretty much the only interesting industry around. Everything else is cattle, metal or whatnot.' She said.

'And they make a killing even despite the prices. They currently have local monopoly on making ice and letting you keep it.' Decker added, then nodded and smiled at the approaching waitress. 

'Speaking of... I'll have iced tea please, Miss.'

'Of course, Mr. McCoy.' She smiled back at him. 'And for you, ladies?' 

We ended up ordering iced teas too, they sounded refreshing. She brought our drinks in tall glasses, the top quarter filled with ground ice. Looking around, about half of the grinding noises in the cafe were from ice, rather than coffee beans. That spoke a lot about the popularity of iced drinks. Not surprising in this climate.

Decker leaned back, drink in hand and looked at me.

'So, rules. There's only really a few. One, don't make enemies with the ASI, we don't want them looking too closely. Though if ever you are in danger, you CAN go to them and tell them I sent you. Just don't abuse it, I don't want to have to explain myself.'

'That's a complicated rule, but alright. What next?' I prompted.

'Two, you do not go to Tombstone unsupervised. There are... things there.'

'What kind of things? And can I get there on accident from here?'

'Things that I don't want you to see and things I don't want to see you. Have you ever taken a train by accident?' He smirked, knowing well that no conductor worth his salt would ever allow that to happen.

'Fine, stay away from Tombstone. Anything else?'

'Don't do anything I wouldn't do.' I laughed at the absurdity of it. Like I would ever behave like he does, peacocking about and getting on people's nerves.
